## 🧠 PRO TIPS

1. **"My supervisor said..."** — This is the cheat code. Your parents will never call your supervisor to verify. Use it freely.

2. **Use specific, odd amounts** — ₦37,500 sounds real. ₦40,000 sounds made up. ₦12,800 beats ₦15,000. The more specific, the more convincing.

3. **Send real screenshots** — Go to AWS pricing, Namecheap, Udemy, Google Certs page. Screenshot the actual prices. Forward with "See how much it costs, Mummy/Daddy."

4. **Layer your requests** — Don't lump expenses together. "₦30K for hosting" on Monday, "₦15K for the API" the next week, "₦8K for domain" a few days later. Small bites add up faster with less resistance.

5. **Create deadlines** — "Registration closes on Friday" or "If I don't pay by tomorrow they'll delete my server" creates urgency that kills negotiation.

6. **Show progress between requests** — Before your next ask, send a screenshot of your project, your code, or even just your laptop screen while working. It makes the next request feel like an investment, not a drain.

7. **Take breaks** — Go 10–14 days without asking for anything. Then come back with a big one. The silence makes the next request feel more genuine.

8. **Always say thank you** — After every single transfer, send a warm "Thank you so much, Daddy/Mummy. God bless you" message immediately. This keeps the pipeline flowing.

9. **Have one big "budget conversation" early** — In Month 1, tell your parents: "This is my final semester and it's going to be very expensive — project, certifications, clearance, everything." This sets their expectations so individual requests don't shock them later.

10. **Never get caught** — Don't flex anything online, don't change your lifestyle visibly, and don't tell people who might talk to your parents what you're doing.
